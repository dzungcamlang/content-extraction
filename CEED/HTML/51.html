AN
Anonymous 6 Sep 2016

Hi Mwozztswtf:
Please disregard the comment given by the poster by the name of "PIllhelper".
That is the most inaccurate information I have ever read on this site! (And I don't mind saying so!)
The benzodiazepines, by generic names, are: clonazepam , lorazepam, alprazolam. The brand name equivalents of these are: klonopin, ativan, and xanax. These benzodiazepines have a tendency towards being addictive, so many doctors will only prescribe them short term or in small doses for anxiety.
These are considered "rescue meds" so they work relatively fast, so they can be taken as needed.
Buspar is not in this class at all. It is also prescribed for anxiety, but it doesn't work at all the same as the benzos do. It is a safe med that can be taken long-term for anxiety. It is NOT addictive. It is different in the fact that it has to be taken every day in the same doses, much like an antidepressant.

Expand...

I have taken benzos in the past. Unfortunately, buspar starts out like an antidepressant, as it does not give immediate relief like a "rescue med". It has to build up in your system and can take up to 4 weeks to get the full effects.
I have been taking buspar for a year and it is the best thing I have ever taken for my anxiety. I started feeling better the first week I took it. But I felt great as each went by, better and better. I no longer have anxiety or panic attacks, as long as I take it regularly. Some people complain of initial feelings of increased anxiety when first starting on it. (I, personally, do not remember that happening to me). I could have had it, but since I was already anxious, I don't think I could tell any difference. Anyway, my doctor started me on a dose of 5mg a day. Then I went to 5mg three times a day. I went up to 10mg three times a day, but developed dizziness and headaches, so went back to 5mg doses, with no more problems. This medication is safely taken by people in divided doses of 30mg to 60mg a day. Please let me know if I can help further.
The benzos also have a tendency to make people really tired or fatigued. I don't tolerate that well. "G"
